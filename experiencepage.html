Experience
My experience in the IT world has been quite fun and exciting so far. Every day, I discover new things and realize that there’s still so much to learn. Before becoming a college student, I worked in a computer shop where I gained hands-on experience. I learned how to set up computers, install software like Microsoft Office and operating systems, perform basic maintenance, and even create simple web pages. These tasks sparked my interest in IT and gave me a foundation to build on. As a beginner programmer, I feel like I’ve only scratched the surface of what’s possible. The programming languages and knowledge I’ve learned so far are still basic, and I often find myself challenged by new concepts. Although I don't yet have a project I can truly be proud of, I see every small success, like solving a coding problem or completing a task, as a step forward. Through my aunt, I’ve had the chance to gain some practical experience in IT. I’ve helped her install POS (point-of-sale) systems for clients, which involved setting up the hardware, configuring the system, and guiding clients on how to use it. This experience taught me a lot about troubleshooting, adapting to different client needs, and understanding how systems operate in real-world business environments. Being a beginner in IT can be overwhelming at times, but it’s also incredibly rewarding. Every mistake is a learning opportunity, and every problem I solve boosts my confidence. I look forward to learning more, taking on new challenges, and eventually working on projects that I can be proud of.
